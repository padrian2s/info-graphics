Key factors (why some countries move faster)

Prompt engineering rewards low-context clarity, trust-but-verify delegation, and access to modern AI tooling.

Software industry scale

Large developer pools and mature IT sectors can adopt agents at scale (e.g., China/US/India lead by dev count).

Education & training ecosystem

CS universities, bootcamps, and continuous learning pipelines make teams better at using (and supervising) agents.

Openness to automation

High adoption cultures integrate AI into workflows earlier (e.g., UAE/Singapore reported very high usage).

English proficiency

English-first models/docs mean high proficiency reduces friction (Netherlands/Nordics/UK lead in practice).

Digital infrastructure

Internet access, cloud capacity, and reliability are prerequisites for agentic development at scale.

Innovation culture & startup density

Risk tolerance, experimentation, and dense startup ecosystems turn tools into products faster (US/UK/Israel/Singapore).

AI policy & investment

Funding, sandboxes, and strategy accelerate adoption; over-regulation or weak support slows it.
