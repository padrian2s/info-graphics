What changes in org math

Execution pods shrink; support systems grow.

Execution pod

Smaller teams do the building with less coordination overhead—fewer handoffs, fewer meetings, faster iteration. The “builder archetype” (one person who ships end-to-end) becomes disproportionately valuable.

Fewer handoffs • higher velocity

Support systems

Platform primitives increase: evals, observability, release safety, permissions, audit logs, and harnesses that let agents run safely. You’re not just “cutting headcount”—you’re reallocating to make small pods reliable.

More platform spend • more guardrails

“Build vs buy” flips (with a twist)

Building internal tooling becomes cheap—sometimes an afternoon. The real costs shift to maintenance, evaluation, and keeping agent workflows reliable over time.

Cheap build • expensive upkeep

Red zone: trust bottlenecks

Regulated or risk-sensitive environments may move slower. If governance and continuous evaluation are harder than capability, adoption decelerates.

Privacy • audits • evals
