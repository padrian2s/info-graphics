Agent teams: convergent evolution of management

architecture

Anthropic calls them "team swarms" internally. Multiple instances of Claude Code run simultaneously, each in its own context window. They coordinate through a shared task system. The lead decomposes projects and assigns work. Specialists can message each other directly — not hub and spoke, but peer-to-peer. This is how the C compiler got built: 16 agents in parallel, 24 hours a day, no standups.

What this means — by role

action items
If you write codebuilders

Try running a multi-agent session on real work, not a toy problem. A piece of your codebase with real technical depth. Watch how agents coordinate. That experience changes your mental model more than anything else.

The skill that matters: specifying precisely what needs to be built, not writing the code itself.

If you don't write codeknowledge workers

Open Claude and hand it a task you've been procrastinating on. A competitive analysis, a financial model, a content audit. Describe the outcome, not the steps.

The gap between what you expect and what you get is the gap between your mental model and where the tools actually are.

If you lead an organizationleaders

Look at the 20 hours/week your team spends on operational coordination, ticket routing, dependency tracking. How many require excellent human judgment versus pattern matching?

The question has changed: what is our agent-to-human ratio and what must each human excel at?

The new bottleneckfor everyone

Technical proficiency → clarity of intent. Knowing what you want and being able to articulate the real requirement — not just your surface request — is becoming the differentiator. The C compiler agents didn't need anyone to write code. They needed someone to specify what a C compiler means precisely enough that 16 agents could coordinate on building one.
