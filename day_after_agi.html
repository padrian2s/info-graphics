Risk & consequence map

Four clusters that dominated the back half: jobs, geopolitics, misuse/autonomy, and “meaning & purpose.”

Labor shock (entry-level first)
disruption risk

The debate isn’t whether the labor market adapts; it’s whether the exponential pace overwhelms adaptation.

  • Near-term: junior roles/internships may compress first.
  • Lag effect: capability can precede adoption → “impact shows later.”
  • Policy gap: institutions may not be preparing at required scale.
Geopolitics & chips
strategic risk

A key claim: if you can’t coordinate globally, limiting frontier compute diffusion becomes the lever.

  • Coordination problem: “slow down” is hard without enforcement.
  • Competition layers: company race + state competition.
  • Minimum safety standards: proposed as cross-border baseline.
Misuse & autonomy (deception, duplicity)
safety risk

Not “doom is inevitable,” but “racing without guardrails” creates real tail risks.

  • Misuse: biosecurity, bad actors, nation states.
  • Alignment: interpretability and control as an active research agenda.
  • Deployment: incentives may under-provision safety work without coordination.
Meaning & purpose (post-scarcity vibes)
human challenge

Even if wealth distribution is solved, the “human condition” question remains: what anchors purpose when jobs don’t?

  • New meaning spaces: art, sport, exploration, community, learning.
  • Opportunity: “capability overhang” lets individuals leapfrog skills.
  • Time pressure: 5–10 years is not long for social adaptation.

“What changes by next year?”

Watchlist: self-improvement

Signal

Counter-signal

Watchlist: missing ingredients

World models / continual learning
Richer internal models of reality + learning beyond static training runs.
Scientific creativity
Not just solving problems—forming hypotheses, selecting questions, building theories.

Watchlist: robotics breakout

Physical AI
Autonomy meets the messy world → may be the next “hard frontier.”
Societal pacing
